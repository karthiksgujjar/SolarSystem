ASTEROIDS - The Robust

Asteroids are small, rocky objects that orbit the sun, but are too small to be called planets. Tens of thousands of these minor planets are gathered in the main asteroid belt, a vast doughnut-shaped ring between the orbits of Mars and Jupiter. Asteroids that pass close to Earth are called near-earth objects.

10 Need-to-Know Things About the Asteroids:

  1. If all of the asteroids were combined into a ball, they would still be much smaller than Earth's moon. The average distance between the asteroids would be about 100,000 miles.
  2. Most Asteroids orbit our sun or any other star in a empty space between the Mars and Jupiter orbit, which is known as the Asteroid Belt.
  3. Days and years vary by asteroid. A day on asteroid Ida, for example, it takes only 4.6 hours (the time it takes to rotate or spin once). Ida makes a complete orbit around the sun (a year in this asteroid's time) in 4.8 Earth years.
  4. Asteroids are solid, rocky and irregular bodies.
  5. Asteroids do not have atmospheres.
  6. More than 150 asteroids are known to have their own moon like companion (some have multiple). The first discovery of an asteroid-moon system was Ida and its moon 'Dactyl' in 1993.
  7. One asteroid named Chariklo is known to have two dense and narrow rings.
  8. More than 10 spacecraft have explored asteroids. 'NEAR Shoemaker' spacecraft was even landed on an asteroid called Eros. The 'Dawn mission' is the first mission to orbit a main belt asteroid called Vesta in 2011.
  9. Asteroids cannot support life as we know it.
  10. 'Ceres' is the first and largest asteroid to be discovered (1801 by Giuseppe Piazzi) and it is the closest dwarf planet to the sun, enclosed over one-third of the estimated total mass of all the asteroids in the asteroid belt.
